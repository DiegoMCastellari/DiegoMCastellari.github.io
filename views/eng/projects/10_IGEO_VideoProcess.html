Road Damage Detection from GoPro Video
With: IGEO
For: Corredores Viales S.A.

In the search for a quick and inexpensive methodology to detect road damages, it was decided to carry out a test by capturing a video of the route, and then process its images through machine learning techniques, managing to identify and locate the damage present on the routes.

Video capture

A GoPro Hero 9 camera was mounted in the front of a vehicle, surveying 50km of route, at low speed.

Once the video was downloaded from the camera, the GoPro app was used to separate the telemetry metadata from it (https://goprotelemetryextractor.com/free/).

Video Processing

One frame per second was extracted from the video, each one being stored individually as an image.

From the telemetry data, the position for each image was determined, by means of the video time correlation, generating a geographical layer of them.

Machine Learning

The images were processed using an object detection deep learning model, winner of the RDDC global competition (https://rdd2020.sekilab.global/), considered as state of the art.

As a result, all the images were obtained with the bounding boxes on them, with their label and probability, and a file with each of the bboxes, with their data and the name of the image to which it belongs.

A grouping of the damages was made for each image, determining the amount of each type of damage and total damages in each image, which were added to the geographic layer.

Quality Control

A random sample of the images was separated to perform a quality control of the result, using the ISO 2859 standard. They were controlled one by one, checking that the detections were correct and that no undetected damage had benn left.

With the data generated from the control, the F1 score was calculated to determine its quality.
