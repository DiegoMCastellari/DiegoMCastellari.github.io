DIEGO MANUEL CASTELLARI

Inquisitive drifter

I'm curious about almost everything. I like the world: phisics, nature, history, space, animals, philosophy; I like music and playing instruments (guitar and cello), reading, playing boardgames and almost all kind of sport. My curiosity always keep taking me from here to there...


The Young Me

I grew up in a small town (8k people), General Pinto, in a rural area. I was a good student (nothing impresive considering the school level). There weren't to many things to do, so I spent most the time being with friends, doing sports, playing videogames, and watching TV. I really like movies and documentaries.

Falling Into University

In 2008, I started the career Land Surveing Engineering at the UNLP. With 18 years old, my expectations about the career and a university degree were realy low; my north in life was really unclear. I remember everyone asked me why I'd chosen that career, and at that moment I didn't know what to answer -and I still don't know-. I'm interested in so many things, that if I have the chance to come back 100 times, I'd a 100 different carrers. The best subjects were geodesy, hidrography and GIS. At the end, I gradueted with honors, being the best student of the year in my career.

First Steps Working

I started my working life as a surveyor in a building, and then surveying electric lines and pipes. It's a quiet, no stressing job, but I always felt there's somthing missing. There was no challenge. After some years, I decided to start the Geomatic Master, to try a career change. The master was the same as in grade career, but I discovered a new path that could be interesting, and that could take whereever I wanted.

Discovering the Data Universe

Doing some data and geostaditic analysis in the master, opened my eyes to the world of data, and after some curiosity research I found out the data science universe. My curiosity keep invetigating, and I don't even realize that I really like playing with data, but that also I could use this in any field I like. So I made a course in Data Science, and from that day I didn't stop.

Nowadays...

I'm living in my small and quiet hometown, working remotely and carrying a simple life, near to my family and most of my friends. I'm still surfing in this data universe, although my curiosity keeps pushing me to parallel worlds, and the doubt of new and/or better posibilities arises. I know I like the spatial data science career, so I always try to merge the knowledges. More importa that the what, it's the why and for what, so I'm still looking for the perfect field of application to work.
