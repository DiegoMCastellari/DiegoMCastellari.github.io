Road Inventory App
For: Corredores Viales S.A.
With: IGEO
Team:
  • Emilio Clair - Project Director
  • Gustavo Torres - Fullstack Developer
  • Antonio Villar - Geoamtic Eng.

Based on CVSA's need to have a system for better management of inventory data, it was proposed to develop an application that would allow the visualization and exploration of data, both vector and photographic, of the entire road inventory, and a CRUD system.

Exploration

A study of documentation and applications of solutions implemented successfully for similar purposes was carried out, with the aim of obtaining ideas and knowledge about needs and requirements.

We carry out an exploratory data analysis to find out which information we had to displayed and develop the application.

A first meeting with clients was held, in order to ascertain the expectations and requirements that they initially had.

App design

The functional requirements were defined, based on the combination of the exploration of similar solutions and the information gathered in the interview.

As the first presentation of the proposed solution, a model of the application to be developed was made, which covered all the functional requirements.

The proposal was presented to the graphic designers, for the correction of its aesthetics and UX; and to the developers, to detect possible logical problems and to know that possible to develop what was designed.

App validation with client

The proposal was presented to the client, for its validation. From the observations taken, the corrections were made in the model and the functional requirements were refined.

Approved the design of the app, the user stories were detailed for the development of the application

Model and services design

With the application already designed, we continued with the design of the data model, map services, webmaps, geoservices, and everything necessary for the development of the application.

Data processing and publication

The data provided by the client was cleaned and processed, and stored in the database, previously created with the designed structure.

Map services were published from views to the database, from which the necessary webmaps for the application were created. A map service for the CRUD was also created from the layers.

With the services, webmaps and user stories, the application went to development - waiting for it to return for testing.

App delivery

After the app was tested and put into production, the customer was inducted to it, along with the delivery of the user manual.
