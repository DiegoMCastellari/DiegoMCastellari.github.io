Processing of a LiDAR point cloud
For: YPF
With: Drone Services

In order to provide spatial information of an area where an engineering work would be carried out, a point cloud was captured from a LiDAR device mounted on a Drone and processed. The processing began after the survey, download and calibration of the data; and contemplates the cleaning of the cloud, its classification, and finally the generation of derivative products.

Process

Point cloud cleaning

A filter and elimination of all the points considered noise was carried out - any point that does not fit the plane generated by the close neighbors, given a certain tolerance - achieving a smoother cloud.

Point cloud processing

The ground points were classified, first by means of an automatic tool, achieving a first estimation of the terrain, and then carrying out a classification by selection. The buildings and vegetation were classified by means of Machine Learning tools, generating a sample of points on which to train the model. Finally, corrections of the classes were made using the profiles, and the rest of the points were classified based on their height with respect to the ground.

Final product

Digital terrain and elevation models, and vector data as contour lines, were generated. The data generated were imported into other GIS and CAD software, such as Civil3D, to facilitate designers tasks.
