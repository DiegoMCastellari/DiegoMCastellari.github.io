Enviromental Impact Detection
For: Pluspetrol
With: IGEO
Team:
  • Elvio Perez - Lead

Due to the intervention of human activity, the natural vegetation in the production zones is destroy, mainly by new roads and facilities, while the enviromental team tries to recover areas out of production. Pluspetrol needed to know the yearly amount of enviromental inpact in the production zones, by detecting the area of natural vegetation affected by human activity and the area of recovered vegetation. The detection and measure of this changing areas was made using satellite images.

Process

Data Adquisition

Due to a short budget for this first stage project, thereÂ´s no money for buying images, so we download Sentinel 2-a images from the Copernicus Hub Page (https://dataspace.copernicus.eu/browser/). For the objective of the project, we used the blue, red and NIR 10m bands, and the SWIR 20m band.

Raster processing

For the clasificaction of pixels in natural vegetation area and human impact area, we used the ndvi and bsi indexes. Using arcpy, we calculate the sum of both indexes, because it separates roads and facilities from natural soil, while the both where a complement to discard areas of bare natural soil.
Then, with the index images was created a mosaic and clipped to the producction zones extend.

Time change detection

The previous process was run for to set of images of different years, so the changes could be detected. Using de minus arcpy function, we get teh difference of both mosaics.
The changes from natural vegetation to road or facility could easily be recognized. By counting the number of pixels and multiplying it by the surface covered by each one, we could get the affected area.
When the operations star, we spect we could also detect the recovered roads and facilities.
